Microsoft maintains a global tax planning and compliance strategy that is administered and coordinated at the Microsoft Corporate level, as part of the Corporate Tax Department, under the Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer of the Company. This design assures proper coordination and implementation of appropriate policies and procedures. 

Microsoft Tax is responsible for Worldwide Tax Reporting, Compliance and Planning as well as for coordinating worldwide tax functions. Microsoft Tax team comprises of groups that focus on 1) Tax Affairs; 2) International Tax and Transfer Pricing; 3) Tax Reporting; 4) Tax Planning; 5) Tax Controversies; 6) Indirect Tax and 7) Tax Process & Technology. 

The APAC Tax team is part of International Tax group within Microsoft Tax. The APAC Tax team is responsible for tax planning, strategy, litigation & risk management for all Microsoft businesses across the Asia Pacific region. The APAC Tax team’s scope includes all direct & indirect tax laws, including income tax, transfer pricing, withholding tax, excise duty, GST, state & local taxes. The APAC Tax team is also responsible for tax compliance, tax reporting and tax audits for the Microsoft subsidiaries in Singapore, Australia and New Zealand. 

The position is part of the APAC Tax team, based at Sydney, Australia and reporting to Sr. Director, APAC Tax, based at Singapore. The position will be responsible for direct and indirect tax compliance, tax reporting and tax audits for Microsoft subsidiaries in Australia and New Zealand. The position will work closely with Australia and New Zealand Finance teams, outsourced service providers and other members of the APAC Tax team. 

#Responsibilities

1. Tax Compliance and Reporting for Australia and New Zealand
• Manage direct and indirect tax compliance processes, in partnership with local Finance, regional and Corporate Tax teams to ensure compliance with local legislations and requirements. Compliance processes include payment of taxes, filing of tax returns, etc.
• Reviewing and maintaining tax codes in system to support appropriate data for reporting
• Timely filing of appropriate compliance reports with regulatory agencies

2. Month-end/Quarter-end/Year-end 
• Provide oversight of month-end/quarter-end/year-end close for direct tax provision indirect tax activities, in close collaboration with stakeholder groups and outsourced service providers
• Drive insightful monthly/quarterly/yearly financial reviews on direct and indirect tax balances on general ledger

3. Audits
• Provide support for and manage direct and indirect tax audits – internal or external
• Manage tax information requests from tax authorites

4. Projects and Process Excellence
• Lead or participate in projects such as product launches, systems integration or implementing new financial and tax reporting requirements
• Support UAT – evaluate testing procedures to meet the required tax specifications
• Identify tax compliance gaps that require follow up or investigation
• Conduct and support the internal investigation of tax compliance gaps
• Drive continuous process simplification and automation

#Qualifications

Competencies required

The candidate should have a minimum of Bachelor’s degree in Accounting or Finance and a qualified CPA, with minimum of 10 years of relevant experience. The candidate should have hands on experience with direct and indirect tax compliances, tax returns, tax accounting and managing tax audits for Australia and New Zealand. Knowledge and understanding of SAP systems will be advantageous
